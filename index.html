About Me 🤓


I am a diligent and dedicated social worker with a profound commitment to serving communities, including LGBTQIA+, homeless, underrepresented, and special-needs populations, through an intersectional and trauma-informed perspective. My extensive experience in this field includes offering essential and theraputic support, crisis intervention, and counseling to both youth and young adults, facilitated through group and individual counseling.

Beyond my dedication to social work, I have a strong interest in sustainable agricultural technology. I believe that the intersection of social work and sustainable agriculture presents a special opportunity to address problems and form solutions.I strive to work towards improving food security, ensuring environmental sustainability, and taking on creative projects and roles that fosters community organizing.

I am committed to merging my social work expertise with sustainable agricultural technology to address the needs of individuals and communities. My scope of work is firmly grounded in solidarity with the intersection of my own identities, particularly supporting QUEER BIPOC individuals. I aspire to take on roles that navigate challenging systems for this community, while also actively participating in innovative projects in sustainable agricultural technology. This enables me to make a meaningful contribution at the crossroads of social justice and environmental sustainability, uniting my passion for both areas to drive tranformative and generational change.

The purpose of this project is to implement a Vertical NFT Hydroponic System embedded in a shelter, providing a scalable and community-focused solution for sustainable food production. The goal is to empower shelter residents by offering a user-friendly, resource-efficient platform for cultivating fresh and nutritious produce. This initiative aims to foster self-sufficiency, community engagement, and environmental responsibility through comprehensive training, resource accessibility, and ongoing community support.

Plant Gene Modification
Genetic Engineering

This report aims to record the genetic modification process involving the injection of a vanilla bacterium into a tobacco plant. Genetic engineering opens up numerous possibilities for the future of agriculture, including increased yields, improved crop traits, and the creation of disease-resistant varieties. By exploring these possibilities, genetic engineering offers a pathway to eliminate factors that harm crops, presenting a promising avenue for addressing agricultural challenges.

PS328 Farm Development - Greenhouse
Greenhouse

The purpose of this report is to document the farm development project at PS328, where a collaborative effort with the community garden "El Coqui," Soulful Synergy, and Middle School PS328 involved the deconstruction and rebuilding of a 10x10 greenhouse. The primary objective was to create a space for housing aeroponic systems, cultivating a variety of crops to provide fresh produce for the students.
